Legacy copper DSL is deliberately excluded from this atlas. AT&T permanently ceased new DSL orders in 2020. Furthermore, across Forsyth County's fiber-built footprint, EarthLink can no longer get a new copper loop provisioned. ⚡ Speed Methodology & FWA Ranges

Wired figures represent advertised plan ceilings. Fixed-wireless and satellite figures reflect typical operational ranges that vary with signal strength, local congestion, and the time of day. Upload is where these technologies diverge most: symmetric fiber matches download speeds, whereas cable, 5G, and satellite are heavily download-weighted.

When it comes to 5G Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) Home Internet, mid-band spectrum—specifically C-Band (roughly 3.7 GHz - 3.98 GHz for Verizon and AT&T) and T-Mobile's neighboring 2.5 GHz mid-band—is the sweet spot. It provides the ideal balance between wide coverage and fast download (DL) speeds.

5G FWA Download Speed Operating Ranges:
1. T-Mobile UC: 170 – 498 Mbps (Premium Amplified/All-In) · 133 – 415 Mbps (Baseline). Broadest mid-band footprint; mobile prioritization applies during congestion.
2. Verizon 5G Ultra Wideband: 100 – 300 Mbps (C-band capped range). Capacity-gated by tower; high stability and rarely throttles under 100 Mbps.
3. AT&T Internet Air: 90 – 300 Mbps (Straightforward Tier). Consistently stable fallback targeting DSL upgrades and rural/suburban pockets.

Why do these ranges vary so much? Distance from tower, building penetration (C-band struggles with concrete/brick), and mobile prioritization during high-demand hours shape home speeds.

Exclusion of mmWave in Forsyth: Forsyth County does not have millimeter wave (mmWave) coverage for 5G FWA. Consequently, even though higher rate plans are advertised nationally, Verizon FWA is realistically capped to standard C-band limits (~300 Mbps) with an operating range of 100–300 Mbps.

⏱️ Latency & Gaming Thresholds

Geostationary (GEO) satellites (Viasat, HughesNet) run ~600 ms round-trip physically, ruling out real-time gaming and causing noticeable degradation on video calls regardless of download bandwidth. By contrast, Starlink's Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites orbit much closer to the ground, delivering a highly responsive ~25–60 ms latency.
